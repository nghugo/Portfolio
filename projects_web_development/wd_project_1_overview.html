Overview

In this project, I created a web form to aid users who are applying for UK Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR), also known as settled status, in planning their absences from the UK, without violating the constraints of their applications.

For a successful application, the applicant must not have been outside of the UK for more than 180 whole days in any 12 month period for the past 5 years.

Based on user input regarding their qualifying period start date and absent periods, the form will inform the user if they have violated the application constraints, and if so, when the earliest day to restart their 5-year qualifying period will be. The user can optionally suppply a projection date to check their remaining absences on that day, based on the number of absences in the past 12 month period or since the start of the application, whichever is earlier.

If the user supplies invalid input, such as incomplete dates or an interval with start later than end, the form will catch the errors, highlight them for the user, and request resubmission.
