Overview

This web form helps BNO visa holders plan absences within the constraints of Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) and British Citizenship applications.

For successful applications, the applicant must not have been away from the UK for too many days during certain time intervals known as qualifying periods. The entire process takes a minimum of 6 years, and is commonly referred to as BNO 5+1. Violation of these constraints can have severe consequences, as it means that the application is void and potentially years of progress are completely wiped.

While the requirements are precisely defined, it can be tricky to keep track of one's absences. It is not easy to calculate by hand how one's previous absences will affect their number of remaining absences in the future, and whether the constraints of their applications have been or will be violated. This is especially the case when one's absences are numerous and scattered.

To resolve this difficulty, I created this web form for users to check if their absences are within constraints, and to project the number of remaining absences they will have in the future. Once the user submits the form, data validation is carried out. This is followed by computation via sliding window algorithms and the display of categorized results.
